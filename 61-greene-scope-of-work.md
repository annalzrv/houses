# 61 Greene Ave — Investment Configuration Analysis

Date: June 11, 2026

## Objective

Find the most profitable legal way to configure and lease 61 Greene Ave. The building can be split or joined in several ways, and each unit can be leased on different terms. The goal is a data-backed model that ranks the options by net cash flow so we can make an informed decision. We also benchmark the property against the best-case opportunity in Brooklyn so we know how close it can get to the top of the market.

All units are furnished, so every leasing scenario assumes a furnished, market-or-above rent, not a discounted one.

## Scope of work

In scope:
- Verify the building's legal status: Certificate of Occupancy, unit count, stories, zoning, rent-regulation status.
- Model the physical options: unit splits and joins, the 4th-floor owner unit, the floor 2 to 3 connection.
- Model the leasing options per unit: furnished annual lease, furnished mid-term (30+ day), nightly STR (owner-occupied unit only), and workspace/commercial.
- Quantify the owner-occupied (4th floor) decision.
- Benchmark against Brooklyn comps and best-case unit types.
- Deliver a ranked recommendation with sensitivity analysis.

Out of scope this phase:
- Legal opinions.
- Architectural drawings, permits, financing, and lease execution.
- Detailed renovation cost estimates (rough placeholders only), but it's the next objective to consider (floors, AC, bathrooms)

## Deliverables

- Building fact sheet: legal config vs. planned config, zoning, rent regulation, financing class, risk flags.
- Market and rent data pack: furnished rates (annual, mid-term, nightly, commercial) plus Brooklyn best-case benchmarks.
- Configuration pro forma model: editable spreadsheet comparing every configuration on net cash flow, cap rate, and cash-on-cash.
- Owner-occupied analysis: the financing, tax, and operational effect of living on the 4th floor.
- Decision memo: ranked configurations and a recommended path.
- Assumptions and sensitivity: documented inputs with sliders for rent, vacancy, furnishing cost, and rate.

## Configurations to model

- Max doors: 6 small units, all rented.
- As-is 5 units: owner on the 4th floor, the rest leased furnished annual.
- 5 units, furnished mid-term.
- Consolidated 4 units.
- Hybrid: one floor as workspace/commercial, the rest furnished residential.
- As a photo studio, artist residency something (i have to check but there is a program), traveling personnel, partnerships with companies, e.t.c.

Each configuration is run under each leasing option that applies to it.

## Method

- Benchmark: gather live furnished comps from Airbnb monthly, Furnished Finder, Blueground, June Homes, and commercial listings. Set rent ranges at or above market given the furnished, renovated quality.
- Model: build a per-unit, per-strategy pro forma covering gross rent, vacancy, furnishing cost, utilities, self-performed maintenance, taxes, insurance, and financing. Roll units up into each configuration.
- Recommend: rank configurations, run sensitivity on the main drivers, and write the decision memo.
