## Reading the numbers

The number of leased doors drives the result. Moving from five leased units down to four costs roughly $35,000 a year; moving from four to three costs about another $36,000. This is why Max doors leads and Consolidated trails.

Mid-term does not beat a plain annual lease here. In this submarket furnished 30-day rates run close to annual furnished rates, so the small premium is outweighed by higher turnover vacancy and channel fees. Mid-term remains useful tactically — to fill a unit during a soft patch through a no-commission channel such as Furnished Finder — but it is not the base plan.

Max doors is the highest-yielding configuration but requires creating additional units and clearing the open DOB items first, and its renovation cost is not included in the net operating income above. As-is is the cleanest path to income today and still outperforms the mid-term and consolidated options.

## Assumptions

Square footage is assumed at about 860 sq ft per floor. Rents are set at market for furnished units in the neighborhood. Operating expenses include property tax (verified), plus estimates for insurance, utilities, maintenance, and furnishing amortization. Financing is excluded; if there is debt, debt service is added on top.
