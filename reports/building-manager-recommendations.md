# Recommendations for the Building Manager

Prepared June 13, 2026
Covers 61 Greene Avenue and 301 Washington Avenue, Brooklyn

## Where things stand

| Building | Plan | Net operating income |
|---|---|---:|
| 301 Washington | Owner on floors 1–2; lease the two 3rd-floor units (2BR and 1BR) furnished | $92,178 / year |
| 61 Greene | Owner in 4th-floor 2BR; lease the rest furnished | $151,529 / year as-is, up to $186,129 at max doors |

Both buildings are held furnished and leased at market. Annual furnished leasing is the base strategy for every unit. Mid-term (30+ day) leasing is a tactical tool, not the default, because in these neighborhoods it does not earn enough premium to beat an annual lease.

## Priorities at 61 Greene

The building has open compliance items that block any reconfiguration. These come first.

| Priority | Action | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Clear the DOB partial stop work order | No new permits are approved until it is lifted |
| 2 | Cure the open DOB and ECB violations | Required before any construction job |
| 3 | File the lapsed HPD registration | Recurring violation; quick to fix |
| 4 | Confirm the legal unit count from the Certificate of Occupancy | Determines whether max doors needs new units created |

Only after these are resolved should the max-doors renovation be priced and scheduled. Until then, leasing the building as-is furnished is the cleanest way to generate income.

## Leasing channels

| Channel | Use it for | Host cost |
|---|---|---|
| Direct listing / broker | Furnished annual leases | Broker fee if used |
| Furnished Finder | Mid-term fill (corporate, medical, relocation) | Flat annual fee, no commission |
| Airbnb | Owner-occupied unit only, stays under 30 days | About 15% |

A note on short-term rentals: under Local Law 18, nightly rentals are only legal in an owner-occupied unit with the host present and at most two guests, and the listing must be registered. Whole separate units cannot be rented nightly. For everything other than the owner's own unit, keep stays at 30 days or longer.
